Project Overview

As Design & Analysis Team Lead for Thomas Jefferson University's Formula SAE program, I performed structural modeling and performance validation of the vehicle chassis, taking components from initial CAD modeling through FEA simulation and formal system-level design review.

The primary objective was to evaluate structural integrity and dynamic behavior under simulated racing conditions, identify critical stress concentrations and failure modes, and propose design revisions that improved performance without adding mass.

Analysis Methodology

Structural analysis was performed in ANSYS Mechanical, evaluating load factors, stress distributions, axial and bending force distributions, and chassis response across static loading scenarios simulating cornering, braking, and impact conditions representative of Formula SAE competition.

Displacement analysis identified zones of maximum deformation under load, informing geometry revisions to critical nodes. Stress plots were used to locate concentration points and validate that revised designs remained within acceptable safety factors.

Outcome

The revised chassis design and FEA documentation were presented in a formal system-level design review, securing a $15,000 sponsorship grant from Tenneco. The analysis methodology was adopted as the team's standard validation approach, and I helped mentor junior engineers in SolidWorks simulation throughout the program.
