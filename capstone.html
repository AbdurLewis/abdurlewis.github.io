Problem Statement

Cleat-to-cleat impacts in football and soccer are a significant source of foot and ankle injury, yet most athletic cleat designs prioritize traction and weight over impact protection. This project set out to design a lightweight protective insert that could meaningfully reduce peak impact forces without compromising athletic performance or adding prohibitive mass.

Design Approach

The protection system uses a Nomex honeycomb core filled with shear-thickening fluid (STF) and encapsulated within TPU layers. This combination was selected deliberately: the honeycomb structure provides a high strength-to-weight ratio and distributes impact forces across a larger area, while the STF exploits non-Newtonian fluid behavior, which stiffens under rapid impact loads and remains flexible during normal athletic movement.

The result is a system that is adaptive by nature — soft and unobtrusive during normal play, but dramatically stiffer precisely when a cleat impact occurs. TPU encapsulation keeps the assembly durable, flexible, and manufacturable at scale.

Test Rig & Validation

A custom drop-weight test rig was designed and fabricated to simulate cleat stud to cleat toe-box impact forces at controlled heights and masses. The rig allows systematic variation of impact energy to generate force-time data, enabling direct comparison of protected versus unprotected configurations and optimization of the honeycomb geometry and STF concentration for maximum energy absorption.
